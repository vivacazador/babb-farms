Our History

A legacy of dedication and innovation at Desert Vista Ranches

Desert Vista Ranches stands as a beacon of agricultural excellence in the Imperial Valley, but its roots trace back to the early 20th century, when Thomas Gargiulo laid the foundation of this legacy. Thomas’s story begins with his parents, who immigrated from Italy with little more than a dream and a deep knowledge of farming. Drawn by the promise of fertile soil and a better future, the Gargiulo family settled in the Imperial Valley, where they brought their old-world expertise to the sun-soaked fields of Southern California.

Through tireless effort and an unwavering commitment to quality, Thomas established Gargiulo Farms, a cornerstone of the Imperial Valley’s farming community. Under his guidance, the farm flourished, producing some of the finest crops in the region and becoming a model for sustainable farming practices in its time.

Carrying the Torch: A Family Tradition

Recognizing the need to adapt to changing agricultural landscapes, Ray Babb and his sons, Alec and Charlie, built upon Thomas’s foundation and established Desert Vista Ranches, a name inspired by the farm’s breathtaking location, surrounded by the rolling dunes and desert landscapes of the greater Imperial Valley. The name reflects both the rugged beauty of the area and the farm's deep connection to the land.

Ray Babb brought with him a lifetime of experience and a deep passion for farming. With a focus on sustainability, innovation, and collaboration, Ray worked alongside his sons to modernize the farm while honoring the principles Thomas Gargiulo instilled. Alec, with his expertise in agronomy, has played a pivotal role in enhancing soil health and crop yield, ensuring that the land remains productive for generations to come. Meanwhile, Charlie has taken the reins of operations and post-harvest management, implementing advanced storage and logistics systems to preserve the farm's exceptional wheat.

A Heritage Worth Preserving

The Babb family remains deeply connected to the land and its history. They view Desert Vista Ranches as more than a business—it’s a continuation of the Gargiulo family's legacy, a tribute to the Italian farmers who first saw the potential of the Imperial Valley. Each harvest is a reflection of their respect for the land, their pride in their work, and their hope for a sustainable future.

From the Gargiulo family’s arrival in the Imperial Valley to Ray Babb’s vision for Desert Vista Ranches, the farm’s story is one of perseverance, innovation, and an enduring commitment to quality. Today, as Desert Vista Ranches continues to thrive, the family honors the past while looking toward a bright future, ensuring that the legacy of Thomas Gargiulo and his pioneering family lives on.
